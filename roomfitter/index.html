Help

Quick start: ① set the room size in Workspace above (or use Draw room / ✨ Auto-import) → ② create furniture in New object → ③ drag it from the Staging shelf into the room — green fits, red is blocked → ④ or click ✨ Fit objects to arrange everything automatically. Step-by-step walkthrough: the ❓ Guide button in the toolbar.
Objects & placing Objects start in the Staging panel (bottom, by default). Drag them into the room and back out; right-click any object for more actions; double-click one to rename it. Leave H blank when creating an object to give it half the room height. Hold Ctrl while dragging to snap an edge flush to a nearby wall or another object (a dashed line shows the alignment).
Confirm: green = fits — click the object to confirm. Red = blocked. Esc/right-click reverts.
Esc cancels whatever is in progress: an unconfirmed placement, or any drag before you release the button.
Alt+drag an object slides it up/down the height axis (up-right = higher). Alt+Shift+drag moves a horizontal surface vertically: an object’s bottom (its top stays put), its top, the room floor (the floor itself rises toward the fixed ceiling), or the ceiling — the lowest surface under the cursor wins, objects before the room.
Room shape & drawing Reshape: drag the floor corner handles (□) — the ceiling always keeps the floor’s shape. Double-click a wall to add a corner; click a corner to remove it. Click a wall of the room or of the selected object (at floor or ceiling height) to type its exact length.
Shift+drag a wall/side pushes that surface sideways, stretching the neighbours; add Ctrl to move it along one axis only.
Ctrl while drawing or dragging corners snaps to right angles.
View, units & panels View: wheel = zoom, middle-drag or Space+drag = pan.
Flat / 3D: the toolbar toggle only changes what is drawn — in Flat view heights, elevation (Z) and stacking rules still apply exactly as in 3D; the height axis is simply not shown.
Units: the ft/in ⇄ cm toggle is in the Workspace section (imperial is the default). In imperial the canvas shows feet′inches″ (e.g. 8′4″); the left-pane inputs are typed in inches (dialogs also accept 5'10").
Panels: the left panel and the Staging panel can each be dragged (by the thin grip strip “⣿⣿⣿” at their top) toward an edge of the window to dock them there, or dropped away from an edge to float freely; resize either from their edge or corner. The − button on their grip minimizes them to a thin strip (click + to restore). The toolbar stays locked in place at the top. ⤢ Layout in the toolbar restores the default arrangement.
Fit objects & auto-import Fit objects: auto-arranges all unlocked objects by priority; review the proposed layout, tweak it, then Accept or Cancel.
Auto-import: “✨ Auto-import rooms” reads a floor-plan image with AI. Click 🔑 Key once as admin to paste the Anthropic API key and choose a short access password; afterwards anyone using this workspace just types the password. Every detected room is created with its dimensions and contents prefilled, and the image aligned behind it. Rooms without a stated ceiling height get the standard height (250 cm / 8 ft). Review the result — sizes and positions are estimates.
Saving & accounts Data: auto-saved in the browser; use “Link file…” to also auto-save to a JSON file that survives clearing browser data. Fit is checked against the floor outline and room height.
Accounts: click 👤 Sign in (top right) and sign in with email + password — your workspace is then saved to the cloud on every change and follows you to any device where you open this tool. “Create account” takes a copy of what’s currently on screen. Offline changes are kept on the device and sync when you’re next online (newest copy wins); the 👤 menu has “Sync now”, “Switch account” and “Sign out”. Signed out, you work in a local workspace that stays on this device.
Keyboard shortcuts & mouse modifiers Tab cycle objects (Shift+Tab back) · arrows move (Shift=10) · PgUp/PgDn raise/lower (Z) (Shift=10) · R/Shift+R rotate cw/ccw 90° · Esc cancel pending placement/mode/menu or deselect · Enter confirm/place (in a field: apply the edit) · Ctrl+Enter confirm/place even while typing in a field · P room⇄staging · L lock · [/] priority · F2 or double-click rename · Ctrl+D duplicate · Del/Backspace to staging · +/− zoom · Home fit view · Ctrl+Z/Ctrl+Y (or Ctrl+Shift+Z) undo/redo.
Mouse + modifier (while dragging): Ctrl+drag an object snaps its edge flush to a nearby wall or object · Ctrl while drawing a room or dragging a corner snaps to right angles · Shift+drag a wall/side pushes that surface sideways, stretching the neighbours (add Ctrl to move it along one axis only) · Alt+drag an object raises/lowers it (up-right = higher) · Alt+Shift+drag moves a horizontal surface — an object’s bottom or top, the room floor, or the ceiling (lowest surface under the cursor wins) · middle-drag or Space+drag pans the view · wheel zooms.

Welcome to Room Fitter 👋

Plan a room and find out what actually fits — in five small steps:
  1. Set up the room. Type its real width, depth and height in the Workspace section (left panel), or click Draw room in the toolbar and click out the outline corner by corner. Have a floor-plan image? ✨ Auto-import rooms… reads it with AI and builds every room for you.
  2. Create your furniture. In the New object section, give it a name and its measurements, then click Add to staging — it appears on the shelf at the bottom.
  3. Drag it into the room. Green means it fits — click it once more to confirm. Red means it’s blocked by a wall or another object. Esc cancels; hold Ctrl while dragging to snap flush against a wall.
  4. Or let the tool arrange it. ✨ Fit objects auto-places everything by priority and shows a proposal — review it, then Accept or Cancel.
  5. Keep your work. Everything auto-saves in this browser. Click 👤 Sign in to sync your workspace to the cloud and continue on any device, or Link file… to also save to a JSON file.
Tips: the Flat / 3D toggle switches the view · wheel zooms, middle-drag pans · right-click anything for more actions. The Help section in the left panel explains every feature and shortcut, and the ❓ Guide button in the toolbar reopens this window anytime.
